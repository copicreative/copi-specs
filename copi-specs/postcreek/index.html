Homepage Rewrite

Prepared for Post Creek Mortgage · Paul Cook
A note on what you said

When we talked, you mentioned your site already converts well, and I believe you. You've got hundreds of five-star reviews and clients who clearly love working with you, so something's working. I'm not here to fix something broken.

Here's why I reached out anyway. I found you by searching for mortgage brokers in Atlanta. Your site came up, and I started reading. The reviews are incredible. But the homepage copy above them is generic, the kind of "great customer service, clients come first" language that's on almost every broker site. So here's the gap: your site converts despite the copy, mostly on the strength of your reviews and your name. Imagine what it does when the copy works as hard as you do.

A site already converting doesn't need rescuing. It needs to convert a little higher, and on your volume, a little higher is real money. That's the difference between copy that's just there and copy that pulls its weight. That's what this rewrite is about.

The core idea

You're already converting on proven demand, people are finding you and choosing you. That's exactly why better copy is worth it: you're not gambling on whether the traffic converts, you're compounding traffic that already does. Every section below keeps what's working and sharpens what's generic, with a note on why each change lifts conversion higher.

Headline
Current
More choices, less stress. We shop 50+ lenders to find your best rate and loan program.
Rewritten
The Broker Atlanta Keeps Recommending. 50+ lenders shopped for you, and one person who answers every time.
Your current headline describes a service. The new one leads with social proof, you're the one people recommend, and the real edge your reviews already prove: you personally answer. Headlines that open with proof outconvert headlines that describe features, because a stranger trusts other people before they trust your claims.
"What We Offer" Section
Current
Great customer service. Superior loans and mortgages. Clients come first. (generic headers)
Rewritten
Why people switch to Post Creek: "I shopped two banks and three other brokers, and Paul was the most competitive and the most responsive." We answer fast, shop 50+ lenders so you don't have to, and stay level-headed when a closing gets tricky, so you close on time.
"Great customer service" is a claim every broker makes. Your reviewers' own words are proof. Pulling real review language into the body makes the whole page believable, and believability is what converts a visitor who's comparing you to three other tabs.
Your Story (new section)
Current
Nothing personal on the homepage, visitors don't learn who Paul is until they dig.
Rewritten
A different kind of broker: before mortgages, Paul was an engineer. That's why every loan gets handled with the same precision and follow-through, personally, from first call to closing. You're not a file passed around a team. You're working with the person whose name is on the door.
People choose brokers they feel they know. Right now your page is faceless until the reviews. Giving visitors a reason to trust you as a person, earlier on the page, lifts conversion because it starts the relationship before they've even scrolled to the proof.
Call to Action
Current
Get Started Today! / generic apply links.
Rewritten
Get a real quote from someone who'll actually pick up, not a call center, not a chatbot. Paul, directly.
A CTA that repeats your specific edge converts better than a generic "apply now." It reminds the visitor, at the exact moment they decide, why you're different from the bank's website they just left.
